Values

What I believe in

Freedom

I place great importance on being allowed to have my personal thoughts and take on things, as well as freedom of speech. I hate being constrained by certain rules that stops me from being who I am or causes me to go against my beliefs.

Self-Respect

I understand my own self-worth and will never purposely put myself down infront of others or conform to peer pressure. I believe only having self-respect can you treat others with dignity and respect.

Happiness

I believe that what is most important is to live in the present, rather than worry for the future. I won't fret over what may happen in the future and just ensure that in the present, I am content with what I have. After all, you can't turn back time.

Personality:

The Debater: ENTP-T

Charismatic & Energetic

People with the Debater personality type have a way with words and wit that others find intriguing. Their confidence, quick thought and ability to connect disparate ideas in novel ways create a style of communication that is charming, even entertaining, and informative at the same time. When given a chance to combine these traits to examine an interesting problem, Debaters can be truly impressive in their enthusiasm and energy, having no qualms with putting in long days and nights to find a solution.

Knowledgable & Quick Thinker

Debaters rarely pass up a good opportunity to learn something new, especially abstract concepts. This information isn’t usually absorbed for any planned purpose as with dedicated studying, people with the Debater personality type just find it fascinating. Debaters have tremendously flexible minds, and are able to shift from idea to idea without effort, drawing on their accumulated knowledge to prove their points, or their opponents’, as they see fit.

Hard to focus

The same flex ibility that allows Debaters to come up with such original plans and ideas makes them readapt perfectly good ones far too often, or to even drop them entirely as the initial excitement wanes and newer thoughts come along. Boredom comes too easily for Debaters, and fresh thoughts are the solution, though not always a helpful one.
